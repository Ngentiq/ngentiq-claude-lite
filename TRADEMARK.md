## Overview

"ngentiq", "ngentiq-claude", "ngentiq-claude-lite", and `@ngentiq/claude-lite`
(collectively, the "Marks") are trademarks of Scott Wilkos / ngentiq.

This policy explains what uses of the Marks are permitted without a separate
trademark license, and what uses require prior written permission. It applies
in addition to the software license (see LICENSE and LICENSE-COMMERCIAL.md).

The goal is simple: users and evaluators can talk about this project
accurately, but no one may use the Marks in a way that implies endorsement,
affiliation, or origin that does not exist.

---

## Permitted Uses (no permission required)

You may use the Marks to:

- **Refer to the project factually** in articles, blog posts, talks, tutorials,
  academic work, and social media (e.g., "installed with `@ngentiq/claude-lite`",
  "built on top of ngentiq-claude-lite").
- **Indicate compatibility or integration** with clear, non-misleading phrasing
  (e.g., "compatible with ngentiq-claude-lite v2", "ngentiq-claude-lite plugin").
- **Quote or cite** the project name in documentation, changelogs, and dependency lists.

## Uses That Require Permission

You may **not**, without prior written permission:

- Use the Marks (or any confusingly similar name) as the **name of your own
  product, service, fork, distribution, or company**.
- Use the Marks in a way that **implies official status, endorsement,
  sponsorship, or affiliation** with ngentiq or Scott Wilkos.
- Use the Marks on **merchandise, domains, social accounts, or package names**
  (npm, PyPI, etc.) that you control.
- **Modify the Marks** (including logos, stylization, or abbreviations) and
  present the result as the project's mark.

Forks made for personal or non-commercial use under the software license must
still be renamed before being distributed or promoted under a different
identity.
